What This Means

Your Foot Shape

You have a Greek toe form with very wide forefoot and heel. Throughout a wide profile.

Beyond the obvious: Given your Greek toes, rather avoid very pointy shoes. These might squeeze your toe tips, even if the width of the shoe fits you. Your arch is rather short, meaning your toes are relatively long. Especially when considering aggressive shoes look for sufficient height in the toe box to let your toes curl up. Your shallow heel doesn't project as far back as most. Deeply sculpted cups will feel empty at the back.

What Your Current Shoe Fit Tells Us

Your shoes range from EU 36.5 to 37 (street size 37), but the raw numbers do not tell the full story because brands size very differently. Your Scarpa Veloce in EU 37 is at street size, relaxed for Scarpa, where the typical downsize is about 1.5 sizes down. You tend to prefer a more relaxed fit than typical across brands.

Your heel fit varies: empty in your Scarpa Veloce, tight in your Red Chili Magnet. Different heel cup geometries; for next picks we target narrow heel volume.

Your Red Chili Puzzle's toes feel squeezed while the other shoes fit on the toes. Your Red Chili Puzzle is built on a different toe form while you have Greek toes. The mismatch is the most likely cause; look for shoes with a Greek-compatible toe box.

What to Look For

Based on your scan and current shoe fit, we target shoes with Greek toe form, normal forefoot width, and normal heel width.

Given your preference for sport climbing both indoors and outdoors and a balanced fit, we prioritize balanced-stiffness velcros or lace-ups with slight downturn and slight asymmetry.

Note: data on shoe heel depth is unfortunately not widely available. We're working on extracting this data from shoe scans, but for now the recommendations match heel width only.

Your shoe feedback was inconsistent across models. We weight the most consistent signal.
