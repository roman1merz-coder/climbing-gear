Section 1 — Your Foot Shape

Your foot scan reveals an Egyptian toe shape, where your big toe is the longest. You also have a narrow forefoot, a low instep, a narrow heel and a deep heel.

Your forefoot is slightly narrower than average (0.328), matching the slim profile of your heel. Standard-volume shoes will feel loose across the entire front of your foot.

Your instep sits slightly lower than average (0.230). Shoes with a high tongue or rigid upper may gap over the top of your foot. Lace-ups let you cinch down across the instep to close that gap.

Your heel is slightly narrower than average (width ratio 0.215) but projects slightly further back than most (depth ratio 0.042). You need a heel cup that is snug side to side but deep enough to contain the backward projection.

With a consistently slim profile from front to back, LV (low volume) shoes are your best match. Standard-volume models will feel sloppy around both the ball and the heel. Your narrow forefoot and low instep give your foot a low overall volume. Even LV shoes may gap over the instep. Lace-ups let you cinch down for a precise fit.
